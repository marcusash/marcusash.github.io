New discussion guide for the next conversation

Three topics. No assertions. Greg's question is at the center. Send as plain text, not a deck. Greg reads in 5 minutes.

Opening, first 10 minutes
Start with the problem, not the solution.
  • QWhat problem are you actually trying to solve with AI right now at ExpertVoice? Name it specifically.
  • QMarcus names the problem Forge was built to solve: one person, more leverage, faster output, without losing quality or control. Does that problem resonate? Is Greg's problem the same or different?
    • Greg explicitly said the deck never named the problem. Start here. Everything else follows from whether the problems match.
    Topic 1, 25 minutes
    How do agents work in a team, not just for one person?
  • QAt ExpertVoice, if three people on your team each had a set of agents, what breaks first?
  • QDo the agents need to coordinate with each other? With the humans? How do you think about that handoff?
  • QIs the friction you see technical, organizational, or both?
    • This is Greg's question from slide 5. He has real thoughts on it. Let him go first. Marcus listens before sharing how Forge handles it.
    Topic 2, 25 minutes
    What would you trust an agent to do that you do not trust it to do today?
  • QWhere are you using AI today and where have you decided not to?
  • QWhat would have to change for you to extend that trust further?
  • QMarcus shares the agent recruiting and evaluation model. Greg reacts: does that apply, or is evaluating agents a different problem than evaluating people?
    • Greg flagged slide 7 as genuinely interesting. This is where that conversation lives. The anthropomorphization risk is a real question he wants to explore.
    Topic 3, 20 minutes
    Show, react, move on.
  • QMarcus shows one thing: how agents communicate and hand off work in Forge. Under 5 minutes.
  • QGreg reacts: does any of this connect to the integration problem you described earlier? Fathom, Slack, Google Docs?
  • QIf there is time and energy, Marcus shows the agent evaluation flow. Greg reacts.
    • Keep demos short. The question after each one matters more than the demo itself. Do not prototype live unless Greg brings a simple problem in the moment.
    Close, 10 minutes
    Two honest answers.
  • QWhat is the most important thing either of us said tonight?
  • QWhat should the next conversation be about?
    • No synthesis slide. No recap. Two answers and done. If Greg goes quiet here, wait him out. He is thinking, not done.
