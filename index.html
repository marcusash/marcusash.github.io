The Team

I managed 6 AI agents the same way
I manage my design team.

Clear file ownership. No ambiguity about who builds what. A shared status board everyone reads before they start. A blocker protocol: try one alternative, then skip and log it. And an overnight brief so detailed the team ships while I sleep.

It worked. Not because the agents are smart. Because the structure was right. And now I know something I suspected but couldn't prove: the management principles I've been teaching for 20 years work better when you follow them to the letter. No ego. No politics. Just the work.

I was the CPO, the CEO, the final decision maker on everything. And I felt incredibly free. That's an experience I haven't had in my career here.
