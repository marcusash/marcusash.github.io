Live Prompts
Compression round (after Marcus demos)
"Before you share your thing: one that held up, one that didn't."
Get specific
"Give me the specific example. Not the category. The actual thing."
Create pressure (use once)
"What's the strongest argument against what you just said?"
Rebalance (if Marcus is at 80%)
"Let me pause Marcus. Greg, what are you building with this right now?"
Cut a topic that's running long
"I'm going to pin that and we'll come back. I want to get to [next] before we run out of time."
Handle Greg going silent
"That pause is data. What's running in your filter right now?"
If It Goes Wrong
One person presenting, other nodding
Run the compression round. Do it now, not at the next transition.
Conversation staying abstract
Ask for the specific example. Say it twice if needed.
Agreement happening too fast
Insert the adversarial prompt: "strongest argument against."
Running out of time
Cut Demo 3. Synthesis is non-negotiable.
Greg is quiet and not engaging
Name it: "That pause is data. What's running in your filter right now?"
Synthesis Block (1:05)
The Prompt. State it exactly.
"We have 15 minutes. Instead of summaries, I want each of you to complete one sentence: I now believe X, and I did not believe that before tonight. Who wants to go first?"
Do not rephrase this. Do not soften it. The specificity is the point.
If takeaways are vague
"More specific. What will you do differently next week because of this conversation?"
