Voice Guide

Six writing rules. Three DO / DON'T examples.
Marcus voice
Six writing rules
01
Present tense. Always.
"We shipped" not "We have shipped." The past is context, not the subject. Keep the verb alive.
02
Short sentences. Max 20 words.
Break anything longer. Two sentences are almost always better than one long one. The period is your friend.
03
No marketing language.
No "leverage," "utilize," "synergize," or "best-in-class." These words signal that nothing real happened. Say what happened.
04
Specifics first.
Numbers before adjectives. "2035 tests" not "lots of tests." "3 weeks" not "a short time." Precision earns trust.
05
No hedging.
"This works." Not "This might work for some teams." Commit to the point. If you're not sure, don't post it yet.
06
Tell the story.
Every post has a beginning, a thing that changed, and what it means. Setup. Shift. Meaning. In that order.
DO / DON'T examples
Don't
"We're excited to announce that our cutting-edge AI platform is revolutionizing the design process."
Do
"Forge shipped 2035 tests this week. All passing. Built in 3 weeks."
Don't
"As we continue to leverage our synergies across teams, we're seeing improvements."
Do
"The QA agent caught 14 regressions this sprint. None made it to main."
Don't
"I'm humbled and grateful to share this incredible journey with all of you."
Do
"Kai got a 92 on his Algebra 2 quiz. MCM worked."

LinkedIn Post Templates

Five reusable formats. Pick the right container for the content.
5 templates
01. The Insight ~150 chars / hook
Use for: lessons learned, what changed after a decision, things that surprised you.
Example opening hook
"I changed one thing about how I run design reviews. The output improved immediately. Here is what it was."
Structure
Hook line (1 sentence). Context (3 to 4 short paragraphs). Key takeaway bullets (3 to 5 items). Close with one action or question.
02. The Story ~200 chars / hook
Use for: origin story, Kai moments, Forge milestones, things worth preserving.
Example opening hook
"Three weeks ago Kai was failing Algebra 2. Yesterday he got a 92. This is how we built the thing that fixed it."
Structure
Scene-setting first sentence. Narrative arc: before, what we did, after. No bullet points. Prose only. End with what it means.
03. The List Hook + 5 to 7 items
Use for: tactics, rules, frameworks, things people can act on immediately.
Example opening hook
"5 things I stopped doing as a design leader. Each one made me better at the job."
Structure
Hook (1 sentence). Numbered list (5 to 7 items, 1 to 2 sentences each). Close with the pattern or principle connecting them all.
04. The Question 1 line question
Use for: community building, surveys, gauging where your audience stands on a topic.
Example opening hook
"How do you decide when to build with AI vs. when to build without it?"
Structure
Question (1 line). Context (3 lines, no more). Call to reply in final line. Do not answer your own question in the post.
05. The Result Metric first
Use for: shipping announcements, sprint summaries, anything where a number leads the story.
Example opening hook
"2035. That is how many tests Forge passed this week. Here is the story of the one decision that made it possible."
Structure
Lead with the metric (bold, on its own line). Story of how (3 to 4 paragraphs). What it means for what comes next. Keep it under 300 words total.
