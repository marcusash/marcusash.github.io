User Actions by Stage (Kai Perspective)
1. First Session
Action
Kai opens MCM for the first time. Sees a clean UI. Starts a practice set. Answers 5 questions.
Thinking
"This is different from my textbook. Is this going to tell me I am wrong without explaining why?"
2. Getting Stuck
Action
Gets a problem wrong. Reads the AI explanation. Tries the same problem type again. Gets it right.
Thinking
"OK so that is why it works that way. I want to try one more."
3. The Plateau
Action
Practices 3 days in a row. Scores not improving. Feels frustrated. Almost stops.
Thinking
"I am not getting better. Maybe this does not work for me."
4. Breakthrough
Action
On day 4, pattern clicks. Scores jump. Sees his progress chart move up.
Thinking
"I actually get this now. The ramp was real. I did not give up."
5. Exam
Action
Walks into the exam. Encounters familiar problem types. Applies what he practiced. Scores 85+.
Thinking
"I knew I could do this. That app actually prepared me."

Opportunities (FD Notes for GR, GA)
First problem must be winnable. Build confidence before difficulty ramps. GR: sequence the first 5 problems to be accessible but real.
AI explanation quality is everything at this stage. If the explanation is condescending or wrong, Kai closes the app. GR: validate every explanation against student-level language.
The plateau is the most critical UX moment. MCM needs to detect "stuck" state and offer: a different explanation angle, a simpler version of the problem, or just encouragement without panic.
Breakthrough visualization. When the score jumps, show it. A visible progress curve that reflects the ramp shape is the most powerful retention signal possible.
Exam mode. 45-min timed session, no hints, full problem set. Kai must practice under conditions that mirror the actual exam.
